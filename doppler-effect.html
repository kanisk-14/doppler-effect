01 — Fundamentals

What is the Doppler Effect?

Core Principle

When a wave source and observer move relative to each other, the frequency perceived by the observer differs from the emitted frequency. Approaching objects compress wavefronts → higher pitch. Receding objects stretch wavefronts → lower pitch.

Master Equation

Complete Formula
fobs = fs × (v ± vo) / (v ∓ vs)
fobs = observed frequency
fs = source frequency
v = speed of sound (~343 m/s)
vo = speed of observer
vs = speed of source

Source Approaching Observer

As the source approaches, each successive wavefront is emitted from a position closer to the observer. This shortens the wavelength and increases the perceived frequency.

Formula (observer stationary) fobs = fs × v / (v − vs)

Source Receding from Observer

The source moves away, increasing the wavelength with each emission. The observer receives waves at a lower frequency than emitted — a redshift in sound (or light).

Formula (observer stationary) fobs = fs × v / (v + vs)

Moving Observer

Even if the source is stationary, a moving observer intercepts wavefronts at a different rate. Moving toward the source increases frequency; moving away decreases it.

Formula (source stationary) fobs = fs × (v ± vo) / v

Sonic Boom (Mach 1 & Beyond)

When the source velocity equals the wave velocity (Mach 1), emitted wavefronts pile up and cannot outrun the source. At supersonic speeds (vs > v), a conical shockwave forms — the Mach cone. The half-angle θ of the cone is given by:

Mach Cone Half-Angle sin θ = v / vs = 1 / M  |  where M = Mach number = vs/v

02 — Reference

All Six Doppler Scenarios

# Scenario Formula Result
01 Source approaches, observer stationary fobs = fs · v / (v − vs) ↑ INCREASES
02 Source recedes, observer stationary fobs = fs · v / (v + vs) ↓ DECREASES
03 Observer approaches, source stationary fobs = fs · (v + vo) / v ↑ INCREASES
04 Observer recedes, source stationary fobs = fs · (v − vo) / v ↓ DECREASES
05 Both approach each other fobs = fs · (v + vo) / (v − vs) ↑ MAX INCREASE
06 Both recede from each other fobs = fs · (v − vo) / (v + vs) ↓ MAX DECREASE

Sign Convention Memory Aid

fobs = fs × (v ± vo) / (v ∓ vs)
Numerator: + when observer → source, − when observer ← source Denominator: − when source → observer, + when source ← observer

Mach Number & Cone Angle

M = vs / v  |  sin θ = 1 / M  |  θ = arcsin(v / vs)
M < 1 → subsonic, no cone M = 1 → sonic, flat shock M > 1 → supersonic, Mach cone forms θ = half-angle of Mach cone

05 — Real World

Where It Shows Up

🚑

Ambulance Sirens

The classic example — as an ambulance approaches, the compressed wavefronts give a higher pitch. As it recedes, stretched wavefronts produce a lower pitch. The pitch shift can be used to estimate approach speed.

ACOUSTICS
🌌

Hubble's Redshift

Edwin Hubble observed that galaxies have their spectral lines shifted to longer (red) wavelengths — they're all receding from us. This is the Doppler effect applied to light, and it proved the universe is expanding.

COSMOLOGY
🌩

Weather Radar (NEXRAD)

Doppler radar measures the frequency shift of reflected microwaves to determine wind velocity inside storms. This enables detection of tornadic rotation up to 20 minutes before touchdown.

METEOROLOGY
❤️

Doppler Echocardiography

Ultrasound pulses reflect off moving red blood cells. The frequency shift reveals blood flow velocity and direction, allowing doctors to detect valve disorders, arterial blockages, and cardiac output non-invasively.

MEDICINE
📡

Speed Cameras & LIDAR

Traffic enforcement uses radar or laser pulses that bounce off vehicles. The Doppler shift of the return signal gives speed accurate to ±1 km/h. Police LIDAR uses infrared pulses at 904 nm wavelength.

LAW ENFORCEMENT
🛸

Satellite & Deep Space Tracking

NASA's Deep Space Network tracks Voyager 1 (now 24 billion km away) using the Doppler shift of its radio beacon to determine velocity to within centimeters per second — across interstellar distances.
